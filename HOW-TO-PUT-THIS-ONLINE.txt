==========================================================
  HOW TO PUT THE MORRISTOWN MATTERS SITE ONLINE
  A step-by-step guide. No tech experience needed.
==========================================================

WHAT THIS IS
------------
This folder is your new website. It has a home page for
Morristown Matters and an event page for Derby Days.

THE BIG IDEA (read this first)
------------------------------
We are going to put this new site online at a FREE host
called Netlify. Your current site stays exactly where it is.
Nothing your visitors see changes until the very last step
(Part 5), and that step can be undone. So you can build it,
look at it, and share it with no risk.

You will do 5 short parts:
  Part 1  Unzip the folder
  Part 2  Make a free Netlify account
  Part 3  Drag the folder onto Netlify (your site goes live
          on a temporary web address)
  Part 4  Look at it and share it
  Part 5  ONLY when you are ready: point your real web
          address at the new site


==========================================================
PART 1  -  UNZIP THE FOLDER
==========================================================
1. Find the file you downloaded: morristown-matters-site.zip
2. Right-click it.
   - Windows: click "Extract All", then "Extract".
   - Mac: just double-click it.
3. You now have a normal folder named "morristown-matters-site".

WHAT YOU'LL SEE: a folder with index.html, derby-days.html,
styles.css, an "images" folder, and a few text files.
Keep all of these together. Do not move files out of it.


==========================================================
PART 2  -  MAKE A FREE NETLIFY ACCOUNT
==========================================================
1. Go to:  https://www.netlify.com
2. Click "Sign up" (top right).
3. Sign up with your email or a Google account. It is free.
4. When asked, you can skip or dismiss any "team" or
   "import a project" setup. You just need to be logged in.

WHAT YOU'LL SEE: a dashboard. It may say you have no sites
yet. That is fine.


==========================================================
PART 3  -  PUT THE SITE ONLINE (THE EASY DRAG-AND-DROP)
==========================================================
1. Go to:  https://app.netlify.com/drop
2. Open the "morristown-matters-site" folder from Part 1 in a
   separate window so you can see it.
3. Drag the WHOLE FOLDER onto the big dashed box on that page
   and let go.

WHAT YOU'LL SEE: a progress bar, then a live web address like
   https://shiny-cupcake-12345.netlify.app
That funny name is temporary and free. Click it. Your site is
LIVE on the internet at that address. Your real domain is
untouched.

TIP: Rename it to something nicer.
   - Click "Site configuration" (or "Site settings")
   - Find "Change site name"
   - Type something like "morristown-matters" -> save
   - Your address becomes morristown-matters.netlify.app


==========================================================
PART 4  -  LOOK AT IT AND SHARE IT
==========================================================
1. Open the .netlify.app address on your computer and phone.
2. Click around: the menu, the "Explore Events" button, and
   the Derby Days event page.
3. Share that link with your team for feedback. Anyone with
   the link can see it. Your real site still has not changed.


==========================================================
PART 5  -  POINT YOUR REAL ADDRESS AT THE NEW SITE
           (do this ONLY when you are 100% happy)
==========================================================
This is the only step that changes what the public sees when
they type morristownderbydays.com. It is reversible.

FIRST, A SAFETY NET:
Before changing anything, take a SCREENSHOT of your current
DNS settings (the page you reach in step 4 below). If anything
looks wrong later, you can put the old values back exactly.

1. In Netlify, open your site -> "Domain management"
   (or "Domains") -> "Add a domain".
2. Type your real address: morristownderbydays.com -> confirm.
3. Netlify will show you what to enter. It will be EITHER:
     (A) a set of "nameservers" (a few lines like
         dns1.p01.nsone.net), OR
     (B) an "A record" value (an IP number like 75.2.60.5)
         and a "CNAME" for www.
   Leave this page open. You will copy these exact values.

4. Log in to wherever your domain is managed. For this site
   that is most likely your Wix account (the domain may have
   been bought through Wix or GoDaddy). Find the section
   called "Domains" -> "DNS" or "Manage DNS".

   >>> TAKE YOUR SCREENSHOT NOW (the safety net above). <<<

5. Enter the values Netlify gave you:
   - If Netlify gave NAMESERVERS: replace the existing
     nameservers with Netlify's. This is the cleanest option.
   - If Netlify gave an A RECORD + CNAME: change the "A"
     record for "@" to the IP Netlify showed, and set the
     "www" CNAME to the target Netlify showed.
6. Save.

WHAT YOU'LL SEE: it can take a few minutes up to about a day
for the change to spread (this is normal, it is called DNS
propagation). Netlify will also turn on the padlock (HTTPS)
for you automatically once it sees the domain. You do not
need to buy a certificate.

7. Visit morristownderbydays.com. When it shows the new site
   with a padlock, you are done.


==========================================================
HOW TO UNDO (if you ever want to)
==========================================================
Go back into your domain's DNS settings and restore the
values from the screenshot you took. Within a little while it
returns to the old site.


==========================================================
HOW TO UPDATE THE SITE LATER
==========================================================
1. Edit the files (or have them edited).
2. Go to your site in Netlify -> "Deploys".
3. Drag the updated folder onto the page.
It replaces the old version. Your domain stays the same.


==========================================================
SWAPPING IN YOUR OWN PHOTOS (recommended)
==========================================================
The site uses real Morristown photos and your real sponsor
logos, plus a few free stock photos for the festival shots.
To use your own Derby Days photos instead:
1. Open the "images" folder.
2. Replace a photo with your own, keeping the SAME file name
   (for example, save your fireworks photo as fireworks.jpg).
3. Re-drag the folder to Netlify (see "update" above).
See IMAGE-CREDITS.txt for which photos are stock.


==========================================================
WANT A CONTACT / SIGN-UP FORM THAT ACTUALLY SENDS?
==========================================================
Netlify can capture form submissions for free. The newsletter
boxes on the site are styled but not yet wired to send. If you
want them working, ask and we will turn on Netlify Forms (it
is a one-line change per form).

Questions? Keep this file. You can repeat any part safely.
